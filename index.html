about

a little bit more about me

My journey into design started when I was 16 years old, sketching digital art on whatever software I could get my hands on. What began as a creative hobby turned into a full-blown passion for UI/UX and frontend design. When I'm not crafting sleek interfaces, you’ll probably find me playing FIFA with friends, hitting the gym, or spending quality time with family and friends—because life’s all about balance, right?

I’ve had the privilege of interning at the Ghana Library Authority as a Digital Designer, where I worked on projects that improved digital accessibility. Later, I joined Turntabl as a Frontend Designer, where I sharpened my skills in building intuitive user experiences. During my master’s program in Siegen, I collaborated on multiple design projects for my school and city, pushing the boundaries of what’s possible with user-centered design.
