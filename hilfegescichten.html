Hilfegescichten

Hilfegeschichten: a website that shares personal stories about seeking and receiving help. It provides a platform for individuals to share their experiences with overcoming challenges, seeking support, and finding solutions. The goal of the website is to foster a sense of community, encourage people to ask for help when needed, and promote awareness of different social and mental health issues.

Problem

The Hilfegeschichten platform faced key UX/UI challenges affecting usability and engagement. Accessibility issues, such as poor text readability, low color contrast, and limited screen reader compatibility, made navigation difficult for diverse users. The information architecture was unclear, making it hard to find and submit stories. A lack of engagement features resulted in low participation, while visual inconsistencies and an outdated design reduced user trust. Additionally, performance issues and poor mobile responsiveness affected usability. Addressing these challenges required enhancing accessibility, optimizing navigation, and improving visual hierarchy to create a more inclusive experience.

Goals

  • Enhance Accessibility – Improve text readability, color contrast, and screen reader compatibility to ensure an inclusive experience for all users

  • Optimize Navigation & Information Architecture – Simplify the site structure to make it easier for users to find and submit stories

  • Improve User Engagement – Introduce interactive elements and refine the submission process to encourage more participation

  • Modernize Visual Design – Create a clean, consistent, and visually appealing interface to enhance user trust and experience

  • Increase Performance & Usability – Reduce loading times and streamline interactions for a smoother user experience

Research

To improve the Hilfegeschichten website, we conducted extensive UX research focusing on usability, accessibility, and engagement. User research and interviews highlighted issues with content discoverability, submission processes, and readability. A heuristic evaluation uncovered navigation inconsistencies and unclear feedback, while an accessibility audit revealed barriers like low contrast and poor screen reader compatibility. Information architecture testing refined content organization for easier navigation, and usability testing improved interaction design and readability. Finally, A/B testing optimized key UI elements such as typography, call-to-action buttons, and color schemes. These insights led to a more accessible, user-friendly, and engaging platform for storytelling.

Sitemap

In redesigning the Hilfegeschichten website, we created a clear and intuitive sitemap to enhance the platform’s information architecture. By organizing content into logical categories and optimizing the navigation flow, we ensured that users could easily find, explore, and share stories. This approach not only improved usability but also streamlined the overall user experience, making it more accessible and engaging for all visitors.
