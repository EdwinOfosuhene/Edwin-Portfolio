SantanFoods

SantanFoods is a modern, online-based food ordering application designed to make discovering and enjoying delicious meals easier than ever. Whether you're in the mood for comforting local dishes or adventurous global cuisines, SantanFoods brings a rich variety of food choices right to your screen. As the UI/UX designer, I focused on creating a seamless and intuitive experience that caters to every kind of foodie—whether you're after quick bites, health-conscious options, or something new to try. The app simplifies the entire food ordering process: no more long queues, no more phone calls.

Sprint 1: Ideation

The UI/UX ideation for SantanFoods began with understanding the core needs of modern food delivery users—speed, simplicity, and satisfaction. I started by mapping out user flows for common scenarios like browsing meals, placing orders, and tracking deliveries. Through competitor analysis and user interviews, I identified key pain points in existing apps, such as cluttered interfaces and confusing navigation. These insights shaped the design direction: a clean, intuitive layout with bold visuals, minimal steps, and clear call-to-actions. Wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes were created to test various interaction models, focusing on making the experience as frictionless as possible. The goal was to craft a user journey that feels natural, efficient, and even enjoyable

Sprint 2: Information Architecture

During the Information Architecture (IA) Sprint for SantanFoods, I focused on structuring content and features in a way that aligned with users’ mental models and expectations. I began by identifying primary user goals—finding meals quickly, customizing orders, and tracking deliveries. With these goals in mind, I conducted a card-sorting exercise to group related features and menu items logically. This helped define the app’s core navigation, including sections like Home, Browse, Orders, Favorites, and Profile. Low-fidelity sitemaps and user flow diagrams were created to ensure that every screen and interaction felt intuitive and streamlined. The sprint played a crucial role in laying the foundation for a seamless user experience, making it easy for users to go from food discovery to checkout without cognitive overload.

Sprint 3: Low Fidelity Wireframe

With the information architecture solidified, I moved into the low-fidelity prototyping phase to bring the user flows of SantanFoods to life. Using tools like Figma, I sketched out wireframes that focused purely on layout, functionality, and navigation—without getting distracted by visuals or color. These grayscale screens illustrated key interactions such as onboarding, browsing meals, viewing item details, customizing orders, and checking out. The goal at this stage was to validate the core experience and ensure users could accomplish their tasks with ease. Through quick iterations and informal usability tests, I gathered feedback early, allowing me to refine navigation, improve screen hierarchy, and remove friction points before moving on to high-fidelity design.

Sprint 4: High fidelity Prototype

After validating the core user flows with low-fidelity wireframes, I transitioned into building the high-fidelity prototype of SantanFoods. This phase focused on bringing the app to life with a polished visual design, incorporating brand colors, typography, imagery, and micro-interactions. Every element—from appetizing food photos to smooth transitions—was carefully crafted to enhance user engagement and create an inviting experience. I designed with accessibility and responsiveness in mind, ensuring the interface was visually appealing and functional across various screen sizes. The prototype captured the full end-to-end experience, including onboarding, meal discovery, customization, checkout, and order tracking. It was also used for stakeholder reviews and user testing, providing valuable insights that led to final refinements before handoff to development.
